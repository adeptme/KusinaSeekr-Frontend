Pork Adobo

Pork Adobo is pork cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic. It’s considered as the Philippines’ national dish because of its popularity, ease of preparation, and long shelf life.

45 mins 4 servings 4.8 (100 reviews)

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Steamed white rice (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Pork In a large bowl, combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and garlic. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight in the refrigerator for best results.
  2. Sear the Meat Heat the oil in a cooking pot over medium heat. Pan-fry the pork pieces for 2-3 minutes per side until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. Simmer In the same pot, pour in the remaining marinade and water. Add peppercorns and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
  4. Tenderize Cover the pot and reduce heat to low. Simmer for 40-60 minutes or until the pork is tender. Add more water if the sauce dries up too quickly.
  5. Add Vinegar Pour in the vinegar. Allow it to boil uncovered for 3-5 minutes. Do not stir! This cooks off the strong acid taste.
  6. Finish & Serve Add sugar (if using) and stir. Simmer for another 2 minutes. Serve hot with steamed white rice. Enjoy!
